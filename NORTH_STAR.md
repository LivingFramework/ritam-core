# RITAM — NORTH STAR
### Constitutional Document · Highest Authority · v1.0 (ratified Session 076)
*Nothing in RITAM overrides this document. It changes only by a dated, deliberate decision (ADR). Every session is read against it.*

---

## 1. What is RITAM?
RITAM is a **governed cognition substrate** — foundational cognition infrastructure on which long-horizon synthetic-cognition systems can be built, so that they maintain **continuity, recoverability, epistemic coherence, adaptive governance, and observable reasoning** over time.

It is infrastructure, not an application. In the founders' words: *"the vision is to create a governed cognition substrate from which many future systems can be built."*

## 2. Why does RITAM exist?
Because **reliability in long-horizon synthetic cognition comes from governance architecture and recoverable continuity — not from raw model capability alone.** Probabilistic cognition without governed continuity drifts. As model intelligence commoditises, the scarce thing becomes *governed cognition continuity*. RITAM exists to build that scarce thing as real, reusable infrastructure.

## 3. What exists if RITAM succeeds?
**A running governed-cognition substrate** — not papers, not insights, not a law. A real, executable foundation where the nine primitives (State, Memory, Ontology, Governance, Epistemic, Coordination, Temporal, Observation, Repair) work as observable, governed components with explicit interfaces, such that:
- knowledge can be **admitted, governed, and persisted** under explicit rules,
- **contradictions surface and are preserved**, not silently reconciled,
- failures are **visible, traceable, and repairable**,
- and **another system can be built on top of it.**
Success is a thing that **runs and can be built upon** — and proves a stated **capability**, not merely "it executes."

## 4. What does NOT count as success?
- More documents, insights, laws, or experiments with nothing that runs.
- A polished specification of a substrate that does not exist as executable reality. *(The founders' named danger: "abstraction inflation disconnected from executable reality.")*
- Discovering or defending a "law." Research is a **means**, never the deliverable.
- Becoming a chatbot, wrapper, workflow orchestrator, productivity tool, memory gimmick, compliance dashboard, AGI narrative, or marketing-driven pseudo-philosophy.
- Code that runs but demonstrates no governed-cognition **capability** (running ≠ substrate).

## 5. Mission-critical vs subordinate
**Mission-critical (the spine):** the operational definition of the substrate; the reference architecture of primitives and their governance; and the **executable runtime** that realises them and proves a capability.

**Subordinate (the support):** experiments, insights, laws, papers, prior-art positioning, audits. These are valuable **only insofar as they inform the substrate's construction.** They are evidence and design inputs — never the centre of gravity.

## 6. The standing discipline (the founders' rule, reinstated)
Every abstract concept must map to **observable runtime behaviour**. Always ask: *What is its executable form? What observable behaviour proves it exists? How does it fail? How is it repaired?* If a line of work cannot answer "how does this move us toward the executable substrate," it pauses.
