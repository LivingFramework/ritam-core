# RITAM — SUBSTRATE DEFINITION (Constitutional Question 001)
### "What is a Governed Cognition Substrate?" — operationally, not philosophically · v1.0 (ratified Session 076)
*Capped at one page on purpose. If we cannot answer §7 ("what makes it a substrate, not an application") crisply and honestly, that is a finding to heed in Phase 1, not a wording problem to smooth over.*

A **Governed Cognition Substrate** is a runnable layer that sits **beneath** an application and holds that application's *cognitive state* under explicit governance. Defined by what crosses its boundary and what it guarantees:

## 1. What enters it (inputs)
- **Knowledge / observations** proposed for storage (facts, updates, results).
- **Queries / recall requests** for what it holds.
- **Governance rules / constraints** under which it must operate.
- **Time / events** (the passage of cycles; external signals of change).

## 2. What lives inside it (held state)
- **Memory** — persisted knowledge with weighting, decay, and recall.
- **State** — the current coherent operating condition across time.
- **Ontology** — the structured representation of what it knows about.
- **Contradiction record** — conflicts, preserved rather than erased.
- **Provenance** — the traceable origin/lineage of every held item.
- **Health/observation state** — its own measured condition.

## 3. What leaves it (outputs)
- **Governed answers/recall** — what it knows, with provenance and confidence.
- **Admission decisions** — accept / reject / quarantine of proposed knowledge, with reasons.
- **Surfaced contradictions and health/repair signals** — visible, not hidden.
- **An auditable trace** of why each decision was made.

## 4. What "governance" means here
Not policy/compliance/permissions. It is **synthetic cognitive homeostasis**: the mechanisms that keep cognition coherent and recoverable over time — admission control, epistemic/contradiction regulation, validation pathways, memory regulation, and repair enforcement. *Governance Before Autonomy (I1).*

## 5. What "cognition" means here
Not generation or intelligence. Here cognition = the **maintenance of coherent, governed, recoverable knowledge-state over long horizons** — what the system *holds, regulates, and repairs*, distributed across the primitives (not a property of a single model). *Distributed Cognition (I6).*

## 6. The minimum that makes it a substrate
It is a substrate (not a script) when: state is **explicit and inspectable** (I3, I7); every held item is **governed and provenance-tracked**; contradictions **surface** (I8); failures are **observable and repairable** (I5); and **another system can run on top of it** through stable interfaces without rebuilding these mechanisms.

## 7. What makes it a *substrate*, not an *application* — and the honest test
A substrate is **general and built-upon**; an application is **specific and end-use**. RITAM is a substrate if its primitives are reusable infrastructure that *many* future systems could sit on — not a single bespoke tool.

**The honest test (carried into Phase 1):** most of these capabilities exist in mature tools individually (databases, vector stores, policy engines, lineage tools, observability stacks). RITAM's claim to be a *substrate* rests on whether the **governed, contradiction-preserving, provenance-tracked integration of the primitives under one coherent governance model** is a real, reusable foundation that those separate tools do not already provide together. If, on honest inspection, it reduces to "orchestrating existing tools," that is a legitimate finding — and it reshapes (does not necessarily kill) what we build. We decide that in Phase 1, with eyes open.
